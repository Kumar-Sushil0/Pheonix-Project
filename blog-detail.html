Navigating the Q2 Earnings Season Volatility

By Rajesh Sharma (Chief Strategist) June 10, 2026 5 Min Read 1,240 Views

As we enter the second quarter earnings season of the 2026 financial year, equity markets worldwide are showing signposts of heightened volatility. Investors are parsing signals on margins, demand outlooks, and corporate spending trajectories amidst a sticky interest rate regime. Historically, the Q2 earnings season serves as a pivotal compass, either validating current valuations or prompting crucial structural realignments in equity allocations.

Across Indian equities, Nifty companies are facing a bifurcated environment. Domestically oriented consumer firms continue to report resilient top-line indicators, but operating margin pressure is surfacing due to incremental commodity firming. In contrast, export-oriented units like IT services and select pharmaceuticals are seeing stable realizations supported by a steady currency, although order books remain highly sensitive to Western macro indicators.

"Market volatility is not a risk to be avoided, but a structured condition to be managed. Proper thematic asset allocation remains the ultimate shield for long-term investors."

— Rajesh Sharma, Chief Equity Strategist

Key Themes to Watch

Our research desk has highlighted three core vectors that will determine corporate performance and market trajectory over the next two quarters:

  • Margin Trajectory: How companies handle minor supply-side inflation without sacrificing consumer volumes.
  • Capital Expenditure Commitments: Focus on whether private capex expands beyond banking, telecom, and infra-related heavyweights.
  • Earnings Revisions: Watching whether analysts downgrade full-year EPS projections or upgrade consensus forecasts as corporate commentary clears.

Strategic Sector Realignments

Given the current setups, we advise against broad-based indices index-chasing. Alpha creation is shifting rapidly toward active stock selection and tactical sector weight adjustments:

Banking & Financials: Large private banks are sitting on robust capital adequacy ratios. Net Interest Margins (NIMs) have stabilized, and asset quality remains pristine, making them solid compounders to anchor portfolios during general market downturns.

Infrastructure and Industrials: Government order pipelines remain high. Companies with robust balance sheets and execution capabilities are well-positioned to convert their extensive backlogs into visible revenue over the next 12–18 months.
